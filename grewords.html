GRE Words

Feb 24, 2015

Apostate: a person who renounces a religious or political belief or principle.
Synonyms: dissenter, defector, deserter, traitor,

Augury: a sign of what will happen in the future; an omen.
Synonyms: omen, portent, sign
"they heard the sound as an augury of death"

preclude: prevent from happening
Synonyms: prevent, impede
"the secret nature of his work precluded official recognition"

Saturnine: slow and gloomy
Synonyms: somber, melancholy, moody, morose
"a saturnine temperament"

ingratiate: bring oneself into favor with someone by flattering or trying to please them
Synonyms:
"a social climber who had tried to ingratite herself with the city gentry"

Feb 26, 2015

Maculate: mark with a spot or spots
Synonyms: stain, tarnish, defile
"Little dirty hands had maculated the lower part of the walls with mud, gouache and colour pencils."

Diatribe: a forceful and bitter verbal attack against someone or something.
Synonyms: tirade, harangue, onslaught, attack
"a diatribe against the Roman Catholic Church"

Waffle: a failure to make up one's mind.
Synonyms: waver, vacillate, equivocate
"Joseph had been waffling over where to go"

Brook: tolerate or allow
Synonyms: stand, bear, abide
"Jenny would brook no criticism of Matthew"

Nexus: a connection or series of connections linking two or more things.
Synonyms: link
"the nexus between industry and political power"

Feb 28, 2015

Accretion: the process of growth or increase, typically by the gradual accumulation of additional layers or matter.
Synonyms: accumulation, formation, collecting
"the accretion of sediments in coastal mangroves"

Anachronistic: Occuring in the wrong time period.
Synonyms:
"everything was as it would have appeared in centuries past apart from one anachronism, a bright yellow construction crane"

Chronic: persisting for a long time or constantly recurring.
Synonyms: persistent, incurable
"chronic bronchitis"

Churlish: rude, ill-mannered behavior
Synonyms: surly, sullen, inconsiderate, impolite
"it seems churlish to complain"

Demagogue: mob leader (who appeals to base instincts), a political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument.
Synonyms: agitator, political agitator
"In an attempt to divert the resulting social unrest, Stalinist bureaucrats and communalist demagogues fomented nationalist sentiments while seeking patrons among the major powers."

Mar 17, 2015

Maladroit: ineffective or bungling; clumsy.
Synonyms: awkward, inept, gauche, tactless, inconsiderate
"This has been an extremely clumsy maladroit approach on the part of the US economic team."

Pallid: (of a person's face) pale, typically because of poor health.
Synonyms: pale, white, pasty, wan, colorless, anemic
"Her mother was propped up against a pillow, her pallid face hardly standing out against the white background."

Dichotomy: a division or contrast between two things that are or are represented as being opposed or entirely different.
Synonyms: contrast, difference, polarity, conflict
"a rigid dichotomy between science and mysticism"

Palpable: able to be touched or felt.
Synonyms: tangible, touchable, noticeable, detectable
"the palpable bump at the bridge of the nose"

Scuttle: run hurriedly or furtively with short quick steps.
Synonyms: scamper, skitter
"a mouse scuttled across the floor"
